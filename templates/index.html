Sharpe Ratio
—
Measures return per unit of risk. A ratio above 1.0 is good — it means you're being rewarded for the volatility you're taking on. Below 0 means the risk wasn't worth it.
Max Drawdown
—
The biggest peak-to-trough loss your portfolio experienced. If your portfolio hit ₹2L then fell to ₹1.8L before recovering, your max drawdown was -10%. Lower is better.
Alpha vs Nifty 50
—
How much your portfolio beat (or missed) the Nifty 50 benchmark. Positive alpha means your stock picks outperformed just buying the index. This is what active investing is judged by.
Daily Volatility
—
The average daily price swing of your portfolio. Higher volatility means bigger swings — both up and down. Compare this to Nifty 50's volatility to understand your risk level.
