Sharpe Ratio
—
Measures return per unit of risk. Above 1.0 is good — you're being rewarded for the volatility you're taking on. Below 0 means the risk wasn't worth it.
Max Drawdown
—
The biggest peak-to-trough loss your portfolio experienced. If your portfolio hit ₹2L then fell to ₹1.8L before recovering, your max drawdown was -10%. Lower is better.
Alpha vs Nifty 50
—
How much your portfolio beat (or missed) the Nifty 50 benchmark. Positive alpha means your stock picks outperformed just buying the index — this is what active investing is judged by.
Total Return
—
Your portfolio's total gain or loss since inception. This is the simplest measure of how your investments have performed from the day you started.
